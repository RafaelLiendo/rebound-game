# Lore: Shadow Cats and the Open Claw

## Core Fantasy

You are the newest champion of the Shadow Cats, an ancient clan of cats who move
through darkness to protect the world from threats too dangerous to face openly.
They do not win by armies, speeches, or spectacle. They win by arriving unseen,
striking at the impossible moment, and vanishing before history can remember
their names.

At the beginning of the story, the player is a young Shadow Cat undergoing the
final trial to become the clan's next leader. The trial takes place deep inside
the clan's hidden base, a buried sanctuary of moonlit stone, old training halls,
secret tunnels, and impossible vertical chambers. The reward for completing the
trial is the clan's sacred weapon: the **Open Claw**.

The Open Claw first appears to be a simple climbing talon, useful for gripping
walls, scaling shafts, and surviving the steep architecture of the hidden base.
The clan master warns that this is only the smallest part of its nature. The
weapon does not merely open surfaces. It opens shadow itself. A true leader can
use it to pass through solid matter wherever darkness gives the world a crack.

The player does not get time to fully understand that warning.

## Premise

During the final trial, alien ships arrive over Earth and broadcast a single
ultimatum: total surrender, with no resistance, or the planet will be destroyed.
Every military response is watched. Every visible weapon is targeted. Every open
act of defiance risks ending the world.

That makes the Shadow Cats the only force that can act.

The clan cannot launch an attack, reveal its base, or gather openly. The player,
still unproven and still barely bonded with the Open Claw, must climb from the
depths of the hidden base into the occupied city, cross the rooftops under alien
searchlights, infiltrate the ships above Earth, and reach the command vessel
where the alien leader controls the invasion.

The mission is simple to describe and almost impossible to perform:

**Stop the attack without being seen.**

## The Shadow Cats

The Shadow Cats are a secret clan trained to protect the world from hidden
catastrophes. They are not assassins by nature; their highest law is that the
best rescue is the one no one ever knows was needed.

Their traditions are built around restraint:

- Move only where shadow can hide you.
- Leave no trace that points back to the clan.
- Fight only when silence has failed.
- Protect the unaware, even when they will never thank you.
- Never use the Open Claw for pride.

The clan's hidden base was built below the city centuries ago. It is part
temple, part training ground, part emergency shelter. Its architecture is full
of vertical shafts, false walls, one-way passages, and chambers designed to test
whether a Shadow Cat can think in three dimensions.

## The Protagonist

The protagonist is the youngest candidate ever allowed to attempt the leadership
trial. They are quick, brave, and gifted, but still impatient. They understand
stealth as movement, not yet as judgment. At the start, they want to prove they
are worthy of the Open Claw. By the end, they understand that leadership is not
claiming power. It is carrying danger quietly so others can live loudly.

The player character can remain unnamed, allowing the player to step directly
into the role. In clan language, the candidate is called **the Next Shadow**
until the trial is complete.

## The Clan Master

The clan master is old enough to remember threats that never reached the
history books. They teach calmly, but never softly. They know the Open Claw is
dangerous because it responds to need, fear, and instinct before it responds to
discipline.

At first, the master frames the trial as a rite of succession. Once the alien
attack begins, the master becomes the mission's distant guide. They cannot go in
the player's place because the invasion has already begun scanning for known
Shadow Cat patterns. The unproven candidate is the one variable the aliens have
not learned to detect.

## The Open Claw

The Open Claw is an ancient relic worn over one forepaw. It is made of black
metal that reflects moonlight like water. Its edge is not sharp in the ordinary
sense; it cuts openings in certainty. It can find the hidden softness in stone,
steel, and shadow.

The weapon grows with affinity. It does not unlock because the player earns
points. It awakens because the player repeatedly trusts it in dangerous
conditions and learns its rules through play.

The Open Claw has three central expressions:

- **Climb**: grip walls and survive vertical spaces.
- **Permeate**: become shadow-thin and pass through solid matter where darkness
  touches the surface.
- **Rebound**: release stored pressure while embedded in matter, turning depth
  into an upward launch.

These powers should feel like one idea deepening over time: the claw opens the
world, then opens the self, then teaches the player to return from the depths
with force.

## Ability Progression

### 1. Wall Claw

The Open Claw first behaves like a climbing tool. The player can scale walls,
cling to certain surfaces, and move through the vertical trial chambers beneath
the base.

Story meaning: the protagonist thinks leadership is about reaching higher than
anyone else.

Gameplay role: introduces vertical navigation, wall confidence, and the idea
that the Open Claw changes how terrain can be used.

### 2. Shadow Sink

The first hint of Permeate appears during the trial when the player falls into a
moon-shadowed training floor and sinks through instead of landing. The master
explains that the Open Claw has recognized the player, but only faintly.

Story meaning: the protagonist learns that shadows are not empty. They are
doors with rules.

Gameplay role: introduces thin-surface permeation. Holding the power lets the
player slip through certain floors or walls with a slow, viscous feel.

### 3. Deep Permeate

In the occupied city, alien patrols and searchlights make normal movement too
dangerous. The player must use deeper shadow pockets inside buildings, rooftop
machinery, vents, and support structures to bypass watched paths.

Story meaning: the protagonist learns that hiding is not hesitation. It is a
way of moving through danger without giving danger a target.

Gameplay role: expands Permeate from a discovery mechanic into a stealth and
navigation tool. The player learns timing, depth, and safe exit planning.

### 4. Rebound

The player's first failed attempt to board an alien ship makes the gap between
Earth and the invasion feel impossible. The Open Claw reacts when the player
dives deeply into a shadowed mass and releases. The pressure of the solid world
pushes them upward like a silent launch.

Story meaning: the protagonist stops treating darkness as escape and begins
treating it as strength.

Gameplay role: introduces lower-body embedded release and depth-based upward
launch. Shallow releases create small pops; deeper releases create stronger
rebound arcs.

### 5. Chain Rebound

Between ships, platforms, and hull fragments, the player learns to re-enter
Permeate immediately after surfacing from a Rebound. This creates a rhythm of
diving into matter, releasing, rising, and diving again.

Story meaning: the protagonist begins moving like a true Shadow Cat leader:
calm inside pressure, precise at the surface, never visible long enough to be
caught.

Gameplay role: supports advanced platform routes, vertical ship infiltration,
and optional assist behavior that automates the rebound-permeate rhythm while
preserving the same underlying mechanic.

### 6. Silent Passage

Near the end, the player reaches alien materials that cannot be climbed,
broken, or bypassed. They must Permeate through walls using only shadowed
sections of the hull, timing movement around pulsing lights that temporarily
erase safe passage.

Story meaning: the protagonist finally understands the Open Claw's promise: the
world is never completely sealed to someone who knows how to read its shadows.

Gameplay role: combines stealth, Permeate, Rebound, moving light hazards, and
solid-matter puzzles into the final infiltration language.

## Story Structure

### Act I: The Trial Below

The game begins in the hidden base of the Shadow Cats. The protagonist completes
the final leadership trial, using the Open Claw first as a climbing tool. The
trial teaches movement through vertical chambers, shadowed shafts, and old clan
mechanisms.

At the trial's climax, the protagonist reaches the shrine of the Open Claw. The
weapon bonds with them, but only partially. The master warns that the claw's
true power requires affinity, patience, and trust.

Then the alien transmission interrupts the ceremony.

### Act II: The City Under Watch

The protagonist climbs from the buried base into a city frozen under alien
occupation. Streets are too exposed. Rooftops are watched by searchlights.
Communication towers are jammed. Human resistance cannot move without being
seen.

The player crosses the city through alleys, rooftops, signage, vents, and
shadowed interiors. The Open Claw's Permeate power begins to awaken, allowing
the player to slip through thin structures and avoid patrol routes.

The act ends when the player reaches the highest building in the city and tries
to board the nearest alien ship. The attempt fails. The distance is too great,
the hull is too smooth, and the ship's light fields expose every normal route.

### Act III: Learning to Rise

Trapped above the city with patrols closing in, the protagonist is forced to
trust the Open Claw more deeply. They dive into the shadowed bulk of the tower
and release the power at the right moment. The first true Rebound launches them
high enough to reach the underside of a ship.

From here, the game becomes a climb through impossible spaces: ship exteriors,
maintenance platforms, docking arms, debris fields, and pressure-sealed alien
corridors. The player learns to chain Permeate and Rebound while staying hidden
from scanners, drones, and light beams.

### Act IV: The Silent Fleet

The player moves from ship to ship, each one teaching a sharper form of the
Open Claw's powers. Alien technology creates moving shadows, light locks,
permeable hull sections, and dead zones where the claw cannot open a path.

The protagonist discovers that the aliens are not destroying Earth for conquest
alone. Their leader fears a prophecy recorded in stolen worlds: a shadow-born
guardian will one day pass through the hull of their command vessel and sever
the signal that binds their fleet. The invasion is partly an attempt to prevent
that future by forcing Earth to surrender before the guardian matures.

The player is not just responding to the invasion. They are becoming the reason
the aliens were afraid.

### Act V: The Command Vessel

The final ship sits farthest from Earth, outside the reach of normal craft. Its
interior is built to defeat intruders: bright corridors, sealed walls, moving
platforms, scanner fields, and patrol routes that leave almost no safe space.

The player must use everything learned: climbing, stealth, Permeate, Rebound,
and chain timing. The final route passes through the ship's shadowed core, where
the Open Claw can touch the command signal itself.

The climax is not a loud battle. The protagonist reaches the alien leader
without being seen and uses the Open Claw to open the shadow inside the command
system. The fleet's surrender command collapses. The ships lose coordination,
Earth's defenses regain their window, and the invasion breaks before anyone on
the ground understands why.

The protagonist returns to the hidden base changed. They did not claim the Open
Claw by winning the trial. They earned it by saving the world in the Shadow Cats'
way: unseen, unthanked, and exactly on time.

## Main Antagonist

The alien leader is known by the Shadow Cats only as **the Signal Crown**. They
command through linked ships, drones, and soldiers who act as extensions of a
single tactical will. Their greatest strength is visibility: sensors,
searchlights, prediction fields, and surveillance grids.

This makes them the perfect opposite of the Shadow Cats. The Signal Crown
believes anything unseen is either powerless or not yet measured. The player
proves that unseen does not mean absent.

## Stealth Rules in the Fiction

The aliens can destroy Earth if they confirm organized resistance. This gives
the stealth condition story weight. Being seen is not simply failure because a
guard noticed the player. Being seen risks revealing that Earth is resisting and
could trigger planetary retaliation.

This supports several world rules:

- Alien searchlights erase shadows and temporarily block Permeate routes.
- Scanner fields can detect motion in open air but struggle to read movement
  inside solid matter.
- Drones patrol predictable routes because they trust their sensors too much.
- Alarm systems escalate quickly, so the player must solve encounters quietly.
- The safest path is often through matter, not around it.

## Level and Setting Progression

1. **Hidden Base**: training halls, shrine chambers, vertical trial shafts.
2. **Buried Tunnels**: old escape routes leading upward toward the city.
3. **Occupied Streets**: low shadows, patrols, and first real stealth pressure.
4. **Rooftop District**: climbing, searchlights, height, and exposed routes.
5. **Skyline Spire**: failed boarding attempt and first true Rebound.
6. **Outer Hulls**: ship surfaces, docking platforms, and moving light hazards.
7. **Fleet Bridges**: chaining Permeate and Rebound between separated vessels.
8. **Command Vessel**: dense stealth-permeation puzzles and final infiltration.

## Emotional Arc

The protagonist begins by trying to prove they deserve the clan's greatest
weapon. They end by understanding why the clan hid it.

At first, the Open Claw is a prize. Then it is a burden. Finally, it becomes a
partnership. The more the player trusts shadow, pressure, and silence, the more
the weapon reveals. This lets the mechanics and story grow together: mastery of
Permeate and Rebound is also the protagonist's path from talented candidate to
true leader of the Shadow Cats.
